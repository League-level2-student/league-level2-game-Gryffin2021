Galactracrusade

by: Gryffin Wilson

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

I decided to make this game because I thought it would be a simple but fun game that provides challenge and replay-ability for the player. I envision the game being very difficult in the beginning, but as the player begins to familiarize themselves with the controls, they will start to develop a skill that may allow them to beat it. In the end, the game provides a difficult but achievable goal of defeating the red ship.

2. What I Learned

I learned that the game may not run very well on certain computers. For example, at home it worked fine, while at the League, some computers had issues with performance. I did not receive much feedback, and therefore, didn't make any changes.

3. Architecture of My Game

The main aspects of my game are the player, the boss, and the bullets. The player can fire bullets at the boss, which is contributes to their main objective of dwindling its health down to zero. This is not without challenge though, as incoming fire must be dodged for one collision could be fatal for the player. The game consists of eight classes that manage everything. The runner is called "SuperShip", which was an early title for the game. The GamePanel class handles the main interface of the game, controlling state changes and what the player sees on the screen. Other classes may connect to the GameObject class. This class handles the non-player controlled aspects of the game. It will manage the boss and the bullets, how they react to different sources, and when or where they appear.

4. Technical Implementation

The game has a very simple gameplay loop. It starts on the menu, but transitions into the gameplay when the player is ready. If they are hit by a bullet, they lose. If they hit the boss enough times, they win. Either way, they are met with a respective win or lose screen and taken back to the menu. An aspect of the game I am proud of is the hazardous bullets. Without them, the game would have no challenge. To finish the game, I started with a basic idea based off League Invaders. I changed some things, and added more and more features as I worked.

5. Most Challenging Part

The most challenging part of the project may have been spawning the hazardous bullets. This was difficult as I needed to develop an algorithm that could detect where to spawn bullets, then determine the direction they are going in and the velocity at which they will travel.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

In the future, I would like to add music to the game. I would also like to add a bigger reward once you won, and maybe a difficult changer. Different scenarios with cover or pick-ups would also be something I would like to add.
